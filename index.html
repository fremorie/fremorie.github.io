After graduating from university I started to have second thoughts about the career path I've chosen. I've spent 2 years working in Data Science, but suddenly I wasn't sure whether Machine Learning was the right field for me. So I decided to take a break to figure out what is it I want to devote my life to.

I love programming and math, but I love art as well. I believe creating something beautiful to be one of the greatest pleasures in the world. Drawing and oil painting were always my favorite hobbies. Art and beauty were the things that I missed while working as a Data Scientist. One day I was sitting at work and suddenly realized that I've spent half an hour trying to find the best matching colors for a graph...

Front-End Development seemed to be the perfect combination of technology and creativity, so I decided to give it a try. And couldn't stop!

I started my self-education path by completing online courses on Coursera, Udemy, Udacity and Codecademy. My deskbook was (and still is) "Eloquent JavaScript" by Marijn Haverbeke. I also completed several chapters of "Hands-on Intro to Game Programming" by Chris DeLeon.
After becoming a little confident with the technology stack, I started to create my own little projects.

The website that inspired me most is the Evolution of Trust by Nicky Case. One day I hope to be able to create something as beautiful and intriguing.
Currently I'm mastering responsive design with the course on Edx and learning about programming natural simulations on Khan Academy.

Physics Memorizer

Physics Memorizer

This is a game I created for my younger brother to help him memorize physics formulas.

The idea is based on the Memory card game (also called "Concentration"): all of the cards are laid face down on a surface and two cards are flipped face up over each turn. The object of the game is to turn over pairs of matching cards.

In this version, each pair represents a formula: one card has a physical quantity on it, the other has a calculation formula for the physical quantity.
